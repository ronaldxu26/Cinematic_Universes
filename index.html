Marvel Cinematic Universe

The name Marvel Cinematic Universe is an American media franchise outlet that consist of very successful mainstream superhero films that are owned by Walt Disney. The universe consists of numerous superheroes published by Marvel comics. The brand itself is worth tens to hundreds of BILLION dollars. The franchise has expanded to television series, new comic books as promotional materials, short films, and digital series. However, my project is to only focus on the films from the cinematic universes. Since Marvel was able to introduce superhero films to mainstream audiences, it is no longer a small market of fanboys/fangirls but films that the entire family could enjoy. Marvel/Disney have perfected their formula for these genres of films. Marvel/Disney did so well that majority of their films have topped box offices for weeks after they were released. Worldwide gross box offices sometimes raked up to the billions for just a single film within the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

D.C. Extended Universe

D.C. Extended Universe is another American media franchise outlet that consist of numerous superheroes published by DC Comics that are distributed by Warner Brother Studios. We can argue based on fandom but the box office scores can tell you that the DCEU had a very rough start with their extended universe. Their targeted audiences in the beginning were not mainstream viewers but targeted toward fanboys/fangirls of the series. This is due to the themes of their comics were much more mature than Marvel’s. The competition between Marvel and DC is relatable to the competition of Coke and Pepsi. Some folks prefer Cokes over Pepsi and vice versa. However, to take a neutral position and to justify for the universes, DCEU has started much later than the MCU with their first film that aired in theatre on 2013 while for Marvel it was 2008. Since the DCEU has not been as successful at the box office and at reaching mainstream movie-goers as its competitor Marvel, the franchises had only grossed about $3 billion dollars worldwide. The quality of their films has been improving throughout the years with their most highly acclaimed recent film to date Wonder Woman (2017) that did quite well on the box office and critics alike. The Extended Universe does not contain Batman Forever and The Dark Knight trilogy films. Those were separate entities distributed by other movie studios. Even though Christopher Nolan's The Dark Knight trilogy was probably one of my most favorite trilogies of all time.

This narrative visualization is a hybrid that follows the slide show and capability to drill-down to the cinematic universes on the third slide. Each slide represents a scene, there are a total of four scenes within this narrative visualization. The slide numbers start from 0 – 3.
